Main geographic distribution of Farming System Archetypes (FSA) in the Mulde case study.


In the Mulde case study, most farms belong to the specialized grassland type (P4). While these farms are typically small in terms of their economic size, specialized arable farms (P1) as well as mixed farms mainly have a large economic size. Horticulture (P2) and permanent crops (P3) farms are not common in the case study area.








Looking at the distribution of FSAs in space, we see that large arable farms (P1) are predominant in the northern and central part of the case study.







































In the higher regions of the Ore mountains, specialized grassland farms (P4) of different economic sizes are found. (© Image:https://www.erzgebirge-gedachtgemacht.de/)






























The intermediate region is characterized by mixed farms that have both arable land as well as permanent grassland.
